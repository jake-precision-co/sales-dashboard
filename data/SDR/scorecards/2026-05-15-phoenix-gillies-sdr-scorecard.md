## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

**DQ exit discipline — stop pitching after you've DQ'd.**

When JC decided Phoenix wasn't a fit, that should have been the end. But Phoenix's curiosity ("what's your service?") pulled JC back into pitch mode. That's a very common SDR trap: the prospect asks about the product, so the SDR pitches. But a disqualified prospect asking product questions doesn't change their qualification status.

The right playbook for "What's your service like?" after a DQ decision:

> "Great question — we're an AI COO platform for software founders who are already running and scaling. So when you have that product live and you're trying to grow it, it's built exactly for that. But right now, sounds like you're still in build mode — and that's cool. Save my number. When you're live and looking to scale, shoot me a text."

That's 3 sentences. Then: "You have a great day. Good luck with the build." Exit clean.

That's a 2-minute pitch to a disqualified prospect.

**What the Precision playbook says:**
When prospect is DQ'd (Offline Business / No Company Yet), the exit is:
> "You've built something exciting, but Precision is purpose-built for software and tech founders who are already generating revenue and trying to scale predictably. That's not where you are yet — and there's nothing wrong with that. When you've got your product live and you're looking to grow, I'd love to revisit this. Save my number."

Keep the product description SHORT and tied directly to why it's not relevant for them NOW. Don't pitch the full feature set. Give them enough to understand what they're missing — and why they should call back.
