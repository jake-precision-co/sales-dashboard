- **Opener was completely off-script.** The MQL call script starts with: "Hey — is this [LEAD NAME]? Great, this is JC with Precision getting back to you on a recorded line. You reached out to us [TIME FRAME] — my job is to understand what you're trying to solve and determine whether Precision is the right fit." Instead JC opened with: "A couple of days ago you downloaded our master prompt generator. I want to see if you got that in your inbox." That's a deliverability check, not a sales opener. It immediately frames JC as a support rep, not a sales professional.

The MQL script prompts: "Can you give me a concrete example?" / "What is preventing you from fixing it?" / "What changed to make something like Precision a priority now?" None of these happened. JC got a soft answer and accepted it.
- **Qualification was completely skipped.** No role confirmation, no revenue check, no business type verification. Matthew described himself as "an operations person" trying to "get opportunities with manufacturing plants" — that's a consulting business pursuing offline industrial clients. Anti-ICP signals fired immediately. But JC never ran the qualification criteria to confirm or DQ.

## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

→ **The authority frame and permission opener are non-negotiable — even on content-download MQL calls.** This call opened like JC was doing Matthew a favor by checking if his email arrived. Flip the energy:

> *"Hey Matthew — this is JC with Precision getting back to you. You downloaded the Master Prompt Generator a few days ago — my job is to see if there's a problem we can actually help you solve, and if not, point you in the right direction. Do you have 3 minutes or did I catch you at a bad time?"*

That one sentence establishes authority, frames the call as mutually exploratory, and gives the prospect an easy out if it's not a fit. Instead, JC gave away all his leverage in the first sentence and never got it back.
