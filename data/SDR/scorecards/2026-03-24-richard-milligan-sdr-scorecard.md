## WHAT TO IMPROVE

1. **Never pitch before you discover.** When Richard asked "What do you guys do?" that was the moment to redirect with a discovery question, NOT pitch. The correct response: "Good question — I'd love to answer that, but to make sure what I share is relevant to you specifically, can I ask — what's the #1 operational challenge you're running into right now?" JC answered the question and gave Richard the wheel for the next 5 minutes.

2. **The permission opener is non-negotiable.** KV's permission structure ("I'm not sure if we can help each other...do you mind if I ask you a few questions?") creates the frame for the conversation. Without it, you're on defense from the jump. JC never ran the frame and paid for it.

3. **Revenue qualification was skipped.** A 16-year SaaS company growing 20-30% annually could easily be $15M+ ARR — above Precision's MQL window. JC booked a Growth Session without confirming revenue. Joe may run a 45-minute call and find out this lead is out of the ICP on revenue. This wastes everyone's time.

4. **The golden question is the call.** This is where meetings are made. JC needs two tailored struggle options for SaaS founders and he needs to drill them until they're muscle memory. The call had no golden question, no current/ideal/gap framework, and no curiosity chains.

## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

→ **The Golden Question first. Everything else second.**

JC's booking mechanics and call composure are strong. The thing that will make him elite is flipping from "answer mode" to "question mode" the moment a prospect says "What do you guys do?"

The drill: Practice this response until it's automatic:

> *"Great question — and I'll tell you everything. But to make sure what I share is actually relevant to you, let me ask you something first. Most SaaS founders I talk to tell me they're struggling with one of two things. Either their data is scattered everywhere and they're making decisions blind — or they know what the problem is but have no system to actually fix it and track the fix. Which one of those is more true for you right now?"*

Richard literally gave JC the coaching note mid-call: "It's hard to assess the value because the ideas are abstract." That's what happens when you pitch before you find pain. When you lead with the golden question, the prospect tells you their pain — and then your pitch becomes a mirror of their own words. They can't call it abstract because they said it first.

KV: *"Be deeply curious. I am not asking you to pitch your software to this person. That is not the approach. Ask questions. Identify a true challenge. Position your software as a solution to that challenge."*
