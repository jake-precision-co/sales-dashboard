## WHAT TO IMPROVE

### 1. Permission & Frame was implicit, not intentional
JC opened with "We have a quick second to chat. If you remember reaching out to SAS Academy..." — Brian said yes, and JC kept going. That worked here because Brian was receptive. But this is luck, not structure.

**KV's permission formula:**
> "I know I'm calling you out of the blue, but if I could take 30 seconds to explain why I'm calling, you can tell me if it makes sense to talk. Fair enough?"

Explicit permission disarms. It gives the prospect agency. It also makes JC sound like a professional, not a guy hoping Brian doesn't hang up.

### 2. No formal recap before the close
JC moved from discovery into the booking ask without mirroring Brian's exact words back to him. The transition was:
> "I'll be more than happy to book you on a growth session, about a 25-30 minute Zoom call with one of my growth experts."

That works — but Brian doesn't hear his own pain reflected back. KV's recap formula:
> "So just so I'm clear — you've got SaaS products that exist and are being used by clients, but you're spread thin just servicing them, and you haven't had the time or resources to build a real acquisition engine around them. Is that right? *[pause — let them say yes]* Why don't we do this — let me get you 30 minutes with Joe, our growth expert..."

When they hear their own words, the meeting feels inevitable.

## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

**Build the pre-close recap into every call, every time.**

JC's discovery was solid. The booking mechanics were solid. The gap is the bridge between them — the KV recap that says "here's what I heard, is that right?" before the ask. Without the recap, the meeting feels like a sales pitch. With it, the meeting feels like a natural next step the prospect agreed to.

**Drill this:** After every discovery exchange in roleplay, pause and say: "So here's what I'm hearing — [their exact words]. Is that the main thing?" Then go to the ask.
