The booking email confirms CEO, but that was Joe's calendar system — JC didn't earn that data. 2. **No Golden Question** — Paul was warm and talkative, so the call worked — but JC defaulted to a generic open-ended question instead of the KV two-option format. Against a colder or less talkative prospect, this gap gets expensive. The golden question forces engagement; a blank open-end gives a prospect the option to deflect. Drill this until it's automatic.

## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

→ **Revenue and role qualification — no exceptions, even on warm calls.** The fact that Paul was cooperative doesn't make skipping qualification okay — it makes it a harder habit to break. The question is simple: "Before I pull up the calendar — just to make sure Joe's time is well spent — what does revenue look like for the business right now?" That's it. KV: "Every single MQL criterion, every single call. Your job is to protect Joe's calendar, not to book meetings."

**What KV's Golden Question prescribes:**

*(Then pause. Let Paul choose. Then: "Why does that one resonate most?")*

The generic open-end worked because Paul was self-disclosing. Against a quieter prospect, this approach stalls. The two-option format forces engagement — the prospect commits to a specific struggle, which makes the recap and booking infinitely sharper.
