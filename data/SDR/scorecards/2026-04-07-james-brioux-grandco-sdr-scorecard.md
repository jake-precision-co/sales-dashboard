## FRAMEWORK REPLAY

**What JC said:**
> *"Most founders I speak with when they're launching, they're kind of, their biggest thing is either kind of converting a client to get on to the platform or finding a client, which would you say resonates most with you?"*

**What KV's Golden Question prescribes:**
> *"Typically, I speak with a lot of CEOs [or: founders getting ready to launch] and they tend to tell me they struggle with one of two things. Either: (1) they're building their first sales motion — no ICP, no repeatable close, they're winging it. Or (2) they have early customers but don't know how to scale from 10 to 100 without it falling apart. I'm curious — which one of those resonates with you most?"*

**The gap:** JC's options ("converting clients" vs. "finding clients") are too similar — they're both acquisition problems. KV's framework requires DISTINCT struggle buckets that reflect real tension: organic discovery (which fits a pre-launch SaaS founder) vs. operational scale (which fits post-PMF). When options are too similar, prospects say "both" — and you lose the golden question's diagnostic power.
