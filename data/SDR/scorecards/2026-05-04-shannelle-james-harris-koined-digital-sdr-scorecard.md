## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

**Learn to handle the email redirect — it is the most common soft brush-off in cold calling.**

KV's response is a one-move bridge: acknowledge → send the email → pivot with one question before hanging up. JC consistently gives up the call when he hears "send me an email." This is a pattern — not a one-off. The script:

> "Absolutely, I'll send something over. Before I let you go — I want to make sure what I send actually makes sense for you. Back when we spoke in January, you were looking into Zoho — were you trying to solve a data visibility problem or more of a systems/automation problem? Just want to make sure what I send is relevant."

That's 10 seconds. If she answers, you have context for the email AND a reason to follow up with specifics. If she says "I really have to go," you have confirmed a real meeting excuse vs. a brush-off. Either way you learn something.

Roleplay this scenario 5x this week before dialing. It should be muscle memory.
