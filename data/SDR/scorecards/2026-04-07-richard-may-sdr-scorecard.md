## WHAT TO IMPROVE

1. **Location not verified — hard qualification miss.** JC never asked where Richard was based. Jamaica is not US or Canada. This one question would have saved Joe a 30-minute call.

2. **Business type not verified.** ECHOS Consulting is a services business, not AI/SaaS. JC heard "consulting firm" and kept going. The qualification checklist says business type must be confirmed every call — no exceptions.

3. **No curiosity chains in discovery.** Richard gave JC a lot to work with — "reduce human error," "strategy is alive," "companies confuse strategy with paperwork." JC acknowledged and moved on. One "what does that look like for you right now?" would have unlocked the real pain.

4. **No day-of confirmation set.** KV's no-show prevention tool. Simple to add: "I'll shoot you a text the morning of Thursday — do me a favor and shoot back a thumbs up." Richard just told JC he had a family emergency that caused him to miss last time. This call was the perfect moment to over-confirm.

5. **Assumed the close instead of driving it.** Richard said "it would have to be Thursday at 2PM." JC accepted. KV: always be the one who proposes the time, even in agreement. "Perfect — let me grab that slot for you" vs. "OK, if that works." Small thing, big energy difference.

→ **QUALIFICATION FIRST. Every call. No exceptions.**

This isn't a technique problem — it's a checklist problem. JC needs to internalize the four MQL criteria as a verbal habit that fires on every single call before the booking conversation starts.
