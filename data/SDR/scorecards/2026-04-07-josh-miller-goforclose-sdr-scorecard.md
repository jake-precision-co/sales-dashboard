## WHAT TO IMPROVE

1. **Never let "I don't remember" end the call.** That's not a brush-off — it's a reset. When Josh said "I forgot about all this," JC should have immediately pivoted to a fresh golden question instead of doubling down on context that wasn't working. Reference to the July email from Matt was a second attempt at the same dead lever.

2. **The recovery pivot is the most important skill for stale leads.** JC needs a prepared reset line for prospects who don't remember past touchpoints:
> *"No worries at all — honestly, doesn't matter. Here's why I called: most founders building sales tools tell me they're either struggling to get their own pipeline moving, or they have leads but their close rate is stuck. Does either of those ring a bell for you right now?"*

3. **Never end a call with "we'll get back."** That's not a next step. It's a dismissal. JC accepted Josh's "I'll reach out" as if it were a commitment. It's not. The closer's job is to create a specific next step or at minimum a question that re-opens the conversation.

4. **Zero qualification in 94 seconds.** There were only 30 viable seconds in this call (after the IVR + context fumble) where discovery was possible. JC used those seconds to reference Matt's email instead of asking one qualifying or curiosity question.

## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

→ **The Forgotten Lead Reset.** JC dials stale leads regularly (old SaaS Academy apps, old Growth Session forms). He needs a locked-in reset line for when the prospect says "I don't remember you." This is a pattern that will keep killing calls.

**Drill this response (30 reps):**
> *"Totally — it's been a while and I called out of nowhere. Let me just take 30 seconds. Quick question: most founders I talk to building [their type of product] tell me they're struggling with one of two things — either [Struggle A] or [Struggle B]. Does either of those ring a bell right now?"*

This reset:
1. Acknowledges the memory gap (AAA — Acknowledge)
2. Doesn't waste time explaining the past (nobody cares)
3. Pivots immediately to their present-day situation (curiosity)
4. Gives the prospect two options and hands control back to them

The forgotten lead isn't a dead call. It's a first cold call in disguise. Treat it that way.
