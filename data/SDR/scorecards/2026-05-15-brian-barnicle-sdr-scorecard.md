## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

**AAA the "I'm good right now" objection — don't accept it without probing.**

When Brian said "I'm kind of good right now. Honestly, like I have more interest than I'm ready for" — that is a soft timing objection, not a rejection. JC said "no worries" and moved to "save my number." That's a meeting that might have walked.

The KV AAA for "I'm good right now / timing isn't right":

> **Acknowledge:** "Hey, I totally get it — called you out of the blue and you've got a lot on your plate."
> **Answer:** "Last thing I want to do is waste your time if the timing's off."
> **Ask:** "Before I let you go, just curious — when you say you have more interest than you're ready for, is it more that things are too chaotic right now to add something new? Or is it that you're not sure Precision would be relevant for where you're headed?"

That question surfaces one of two things:
- A timing objection → "Okay, so if you're thinking things will open up in 30-60 days, would it make sense for me to reach back out then? I'll set a reminder."
- A need/fit question → "Got it — what would need to be true about Precision for it to be worth a 30-min conversation?"

Either answer gives JC something to work with. "No worries, save my number" gives him nothing.
