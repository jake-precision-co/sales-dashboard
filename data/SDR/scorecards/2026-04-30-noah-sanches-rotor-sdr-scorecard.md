## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

→ **Learn and execute the AAA framework before the next session.**

When Noah said "probably not this time," JC had a choice: AAA or pitch. He pitched. That's the most dangerous habit an SDR can have — using pitch as a brush-off handler. Here's what it should have looked like:

**What JC said:**
> "Uh, curious stuff, potentially, you're open to a conversation... kind of, so I'll kind of give you a, again, this is not a pitch by any means, but I'll give you kind of like a quick update..."

**KV AAA response:**
> "Oh, totally get it — I called you out of the blue. Last thing I want to do is waste your time. Before I let you go — quick question. Most of the founders I talk to are struggling with one of two things: either they're doing everything themselves and they can't see where growth is stalling, or they're flying blind on the numbers and making gut-feel decisions they're not sure about. Which of those sounds more like where you're at right now?"

That's it. Acknowledge. Answer. Ask. The golden question is the Ask. That's the full framework in one breath — and it earns the next five minutes.
