→ **Convert warm moments into qualified intel — don't let rapport die without a golden question.**

JC created a genuinely great moment with the Ferrari. Justin was warm and open. And then JC launched a pitch and texted a Calendly link. That's the equivalent of getting a great first base hit and stopping to wave at the crowd.

**What JC said after the car rapport:**
> "I don't want to take up too much of your time. I'd love to say something up just a two-minute chat. Just to see if something you're still open having a conversation about..."

**What KV + framework prescribes:**
> "Right — and I don't want to keep you while you're driving. Quick question since you're already on the line: you mentioned you need to get back to scaling sales. Most founders at that stage are either dealing with [A] a pipeline that's too thin and not predictable, or [B] a sales process that's not converting what they're generating. Which of those sounds more like your situation right now?"

Then: "Okay — let me send you a calendar invite right now. Takes 30 seconds. What's the best email? And do mornings or afternoons tend to work better for you?"

That sequence extracts intel AND locks the booking with specificity — all while Justin's still on the phone. The Calendly link is the version where JC hopes Justin remembers to book. An invite sent on the call is the version where it actually happens.
