## WHAT TO IMPROVE

- **Opener clarity** — This is the #1 issue. JC, you've got 3 seconds. Name. Company. Reason. In that order, clean. "Hey Seth, it's JC over at Precision — you jumped on our radar a while back and I was reaching out to see if anything's changed." That's it. Drill this. If the first sentence confuses the prospect, you've already lost.
- **Stop double-apologizing** — One acknowledgment, done. "My apologies" once is enough. Saying it twice sounds like you're asking for forgiveness for existing. You don't need permission to make a call. Own the dial.
- **Leave one door open before removing** — "Totally fair — 7 years is a long time. Before I close this out, real quick: is this just not relevant for you right now, or just not ever?" It's one question. 10 seconds. Sometimes it turns into a referral. Sometimes it turns into "actually, let me ask you one thing." You'll never know if you don't ask.

## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

**Opener clarity under pressure.** This call died in the first 15 seconds because the opener was tangled. When you're calling on stale leads or re-engagement outreach, the opener requires MORE precision, not less. The prospect doesn't remember you. They don't know "SaaS Academy became Precision." Your first sentence has to orient them instantly: who you are, what company, and one sentence of context for why you're calling. Practice the opener out loud, on camera, until it sounds like you've said it 10,000 times — because you should have.
