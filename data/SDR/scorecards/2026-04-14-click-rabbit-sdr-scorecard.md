## TOP COACHING PRIORITY

**→ Book THEN Discover is backwards. Discovery CREATES the booking.**

JC's pattern on booked calls: prospect is warm → JC pitches the value → prospect agrees to book → JC asks "what do you want to get out of this?" This is the reverse of the KV framework.

The golden question should come BEFORE the booking ask, not after. The pain gap IS the reason for the meeting. When JC asks about Noah's "consistency in deal flow" BEFORE booking, he can say: "That's exactly what we help with. Here's why a 25-minute call with Joe makes sense right now — he works with founders at this exact stage..."

**What JC said:**
> "As I send this over... in a perfect world, what else would you kind of hope to improve or maybe something to get out of this call?"

**What KV prescribes (golden question → booking, not booking → golden question):**
> "Quick question before we look at the calendar — what's the biggest thing holding you back on [deal flow / scaling your partnerships] right now? Is it more around [option A: getting consistent volume in without relying on referrals] or [option B: building out the backend infrastructure to handle more white-label volume]?" 
> 
> *[Prospect picks one]*
> 
> "That's exactly what Joe focuses on. The Growth Session is built for this — 25 minutes, he's worked with 40+ companies at your stage. How does Thursday at 2 work?"

This structure makes the booking feel inevitable because it's a direct response to Noah's stated pain — not a general invitation.
