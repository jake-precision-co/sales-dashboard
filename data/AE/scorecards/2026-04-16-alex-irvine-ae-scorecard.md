## WHAT TO IMPROVE

### 1. ❌ NO DREAM UNCOVERED — Skipped Entire Phase 2
**What happened:** Joe identified the gap (developer problem, outside Precision's scope) and DQ'd without ever asking: "Alex, if you did get this fixed and launched — what does this do for you? What does your business look like a year from now?" This section scored 2/15.
**Why it matters:** Discovery is not just qualification — it's intelligence. Even on a DQ, understanding Alex's vision tells Joe: Is this a referral? A future client? Someone for the Precision network? Joe ended the call knowing LESS about Alex than he should.
**DM principle:** "Check first. Check details. Make sure the person you're DQ'ing isn't actually the right person in the wrong conversation."
**Script:** *"Alex, before I give you my honest take on fit — what's the dream here? You've clearly invested in this. Where are you trying to take it? What does it do for you when it works?"*

### 2. ❌ "WHY US" / "WHY NOW" QUESTIONS MISSING — Qualify Section
**What happened:** Joe never asked what triggered Alex to book this call, or why Precision specifically (vs. finding a developer themselves). Qualify scored 5/15.
**Impact:** Without a trigger, Joe doesn't know the urgency level. Alex's timeline is unclear. Is this "I need to fix this in 2 weeks" or "casual exploration"? Joe never found out.
**Script:** *"Alex, what made you book this call now? What's the timeline on fixing this and launching?"*

### 3. ❌ NO BAMFAM — Even DQ Calls Need a Next Step
**What happened:** Joe said "I'll do some legwork here internally and if I do get anything good for you, I'll fire it over." That's not a calendar'd next step.
**DM prescribes:** Book a specific follow-up. *"Let me pull those materials together. I'll send them over today. And honestly, once you get a developer and have a product — let's reconnect. I'll put a reminder in for 60 days. Where do you expect to be by mid-June?"* Calendar it. Don't leave it open-ended.

### 4. ❌ CONSEQUENCE CHECK MISSING
**What happened:** Joe never asked "What happens if this product stays broken? What's the cost of another 3 months with an unreliable developer?" The stakes were never explored.
**Script (JM NEPQ):** *"Alex, sounds like you've already lost time on this. If the developer situation stays unresolved, what does that look like for you in 6 months? What does that cost you?"*
