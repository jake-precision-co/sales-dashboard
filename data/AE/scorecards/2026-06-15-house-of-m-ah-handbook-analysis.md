### 3. Obstacle ⚠️

**Handbook standard:** *"Current obstacle - What's your biggest roadblock right now? What's holding you back?"*

### 4. Past (What have you tried?) ❌
Joe never asks. "What have you done so far to solve this?" is a core discovery question that reveals buying readiness and justifies urgency. Joe skipped it entirely. He doesn't know what Ro has already tried or spent money on.

### 5. Reason (Why it failed) ❌
Joe never digs into root cause. He identifies the symptoms (on fire, stuck in field, bad at HR) but never asks "Why is that? What's the root cause?" Handbook: "Why? Example?" — This is what builds real pain.

He never says: "So let me make sure I understand — you're in the field when you should be on the business, HR is all on you, cash flow is inconsistent, leads are all outbound, and if nothing changes, you stay stuck in the middle. Does that sound right?"

The handbook calls this "stacking the pain" — it's mandatory before the offer. Joe goes straight to pitching without it.

**Handbook standard:**  
> "Recap - To make sure I understand. You tried three different marketing agencies, spent $50K, got leads that didn't convert, and now you're hesitant to invest again. Label - So it sounds like the real issue isn't just vendors, ads, and leads - it's that you need an entirely new marketing and sales process. Confirm - Does that sound right?"

### 7. Unique (Confirm + Label) ❌
Joe does minimal labeling. He never maps Ro's problems to Precision's solution categories (People, Operations, Marketing, Sales, Profit) before transitioning to the offer. He just pivots to demo mode.
