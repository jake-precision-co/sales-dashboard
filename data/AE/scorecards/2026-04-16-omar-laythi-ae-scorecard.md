## WHAT TO IMPROVE

### 1. ❌ NO BAMFAM — Cardinal Sin
**What happened:** Joe closed with "You know where to find us. When the time is right, don't hesitate to reach out." Zero specific next step. Omar said "Everybody wants to. Only a few actually do."
**What DM prescribes:** *"No call ends without a calendar'd next step. Zero exceptions."* Even on a defer, Joe should have said: "Let's put something on for May 15th — 30 days. I'll check in on how the product's coming. That way I'm not just another guy you talked to once." Calendar the conversation. Lock the follow-up.
**Impact:** Joe is relying on Omar to reach out. He won't. Lost pipeline that could close in 60–90 days.

### 2. ❌ CONSEQUENCE CHECK MISSING — JM NEPQ Gap
**What happened:** Joe never asked anything like "What happens if you don't get into these rooms and figure this out yourself over the next year?" or "What does that cost you in time and speed?"
**What JM prescribes:** *"When prospects come to their own conclusions, they will fight for them."* Omar WANTED to be pushed. He literally said "I want them to call me on my sh*t." Joe had the perfect setup and never asked the consequence question.
**Missed script:** *"Omar, you said you want to win. What happens if you spend the next 12 months grinding this out solo, making all the mistakes that other founders already solved? What does that cost you?"*

### 3. ❌ DREAM ANCHOR UNDER-DEVELOPED
**What happened:** Omar's dream (business coach after multiple SaaS exits) came out organically at [261s], but Joe never returned to anchor it emotionally during the pitch or close. He said "I love the vision" and moved on.
**What Precision Framework prescribes:** Dream → Gap → Guide → Offer. The dream should fuel the close. Joe should have looped back: *"Omar, you said your dream is to be a business coach who's actually done it. Every month you're not in a room with people who've already exited SaaS companies is a month you're not shortening that path. That's the real cost."*
**AH Closer:** "Looping back to discovery creates urgency. The dream IS the reason to buy."
